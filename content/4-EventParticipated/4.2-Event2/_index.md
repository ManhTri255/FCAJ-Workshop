# Summary Report – GenAI-powered App-DB Modernization Workshop

### I. Purpose and Significance of Attending the Event

Participating in the **GenAI-powered App-DB Modernization** workshop gave me the opportunity to gain a broader perspective on how modern software systems are being designed and evolved. Beyond the technical content, the event also helped me better understand how organizations approach system modernization, data strategy, and operational efficiency in a rapidly changing technology landscape.

### II. Speaker List

- **Jignesh Shah** – Director, Open Source Databases
- **Erica Liu** – Sr. GTM Specialist, AppMod
- **Fabrianne Effendi** – Assc. Specialist SA, Serverless Amazon Web Services

### III. Key Highlights from the Sessions

#### 1. Identifying the drawbacks of legacy application architecture

- Long product release cycles often lead to lost opportunities and revenue
- Inefficient operations increase costs and reduce productivity
- Failure to meet security standards can damage trust and expose the business to risk

#### 2. Transitioning to modern application architecture – Microservices

The workshop showed that moving to a modular system is not only about splitting features into smaller parts, but also about creating clear communication between components. Each capability can operate as an **independent service**, exchange information through **events**, and rely on three core pillars:

- **Queue Management**: Handling asynchronous tasks efficiently
- **Caching Strategy**: Improving performance by reducing system load
- **Message Handling**: Enabling flexible communication between services

#### 3. Domain-Driven Design (DDD)

- **Four-step method**: Define domain events, arrange the timeline, identify actors, and define bounded contexts
- **Bookstore case study**: Demonstrates how DDD can be applied in real-world scenarios
- **Context mapping**: Using 7 patterns to connect bounded contexts in an organized way

#### 4. Event-Driven Architecture

- **3 integration patterns**: Publish/Subscribe, Point-to-point, and Streaming
- **Benefits**: Reducing tight coupling, improving scalability, and increasing resilience
- **Sync vs async comparison**: Helping participants understand when each approach is appropriate

#### 5. Compute Evolution

- **Shared Responsibility Model**: From EC2 to ECS, Fargate, and Lambda
- **Serverless benefits**: Eliminating server management, scaling automatically, and paying only for actual usage
- **Functions vs Containers**: Choosing the right technology for different workloads

#### 6. Amazon Q Developer

- **SDLC automation**: Supporting work from planning through maintenance
- **Code transformation**: Assisting with Java upgrades and .NET modernization
- **AWS Transform agents**: Useful for VMware, Mainframe, and .NET migration scenarios

### IV. Knowledge and Skills Gained

#### Design Mindset

- **Business-first approach**: Placing business needs ahead of technology
- **Ubiquitous language**: The importance of a shared vocabulary between business and technical teams
- **Bounded contexts**: Managing complexity in large systems by dividing them into well-defined areas

#### Technical Architecture

- **Event storming technique**: A practical way to model business processes
- Using **event-driven communication** instead of traditional synchronous calls
- **Integration patterns**: Understanding when to use sync, async, pub/sub, or streaming
- **Compute spectrum**: Choosing between VM, containers, and serverless based on goals

#### Modernization Strategy

- **Phased approach**: Avoiding rushed transformation and following a clear roadmap
- **7Rs framework**: Offering several modernization paths suited to different applications
- **ROI measurement**: Evaluating impact through cost reduction and greater business agility

### V. Application Direction for Study and Work

- **Apply DDD** to current projects through event storming sessions with business teams
- **Refactor microservices** by using bounded contexts to define service boundaries more clearly
- **Implement event-driven patterns** by replacing some synchronous communication with async messaging
- **Adopt serverless** by testing AWS Lambda for suitable use cases
- **Try Amazon Q Developer** by integrating it into the development workflow to improve efficiency

### VI. Real-World Event Experience

#### Learning from highly skilled speakers

- The speakers from AWS and other major technology organizations offered many practical perspectives on building modern applications.
- Through the case studies, I gained a clearer understanding of how to apply **Domain-Driven Design (DDD)** and **Event-Driven Architecture** in large-scale projects.

#### Hands-on technical exposure

- The discussions around **event storming** helped me visualize how to turn business processes into meaningful domain events.
- I also developed a better understanding of how to break down systems into **microservices** and use **bounded contexts** to control complexity.
- The difference between **synchronous and asynchronous communication**, along with patterns such as **pub/sub, point-to-point, and streaming**, became much clearer.

#### Leveraging modern tools

- I had the chance to learn more about **Amazon Q Developer**, an AI tool that supports multiple stages of the SDLC.
- The session also highlighted the value of automating **code transformation** and running serverless workloads with **AWS Lambda**.

#### Networking and discussions

- The event created opportunities for me to exchange ideas directly with experts, colleagues, and members of the business team.
- The real-world examples reinforced that building good systems is not only about technology, but also about shared understanding across stakeholders.

### VII. Lessons Learned After the Event

The biggest lesson was that modernization requires both technical insight and a clear strategic roadmap. Applying DDD and event-driven patterns makes systems more flexible, reduces unnecessary dependence, and improves adaptability. At the same time, AI tools such as Amazon Q Developer can bring significant benefits when used in the right context and for the right purpose.
