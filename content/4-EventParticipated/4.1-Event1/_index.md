# Detailed Summary Report – FCAJ Community Day

### I. Purpose and Significance of Attending the Event

Participating in the **FCAJ Community Day** workshop gave me a valuable opportunity to access and update the latest technology trends, especially in **Cloud Computing** and **Generative AI**.

The event was not only a place for theoretical sharing, but also a practical environment where I could learn from real-world experiences, understand modern application development processes, and see how experts apply technology in practice. Through the in-depth discussions, I gained a clearer understanding of how businesses optimize workflows with AI, leverage advanced AWS services, and shape a more defined future career path.

### II. Guest Speaker List

- **Tinh Truong** – Speaker specializing in AI & Context Engineering
- **Anh Pham** – Speaker presenting the Amazon Quick solution
- **Thinh Nguyen** – Speaker specializing in infrastructure optimization with Amazon CloudFront
- **Team VIB** – Talented team from the LotusHacks Sprint Hackathon
- **Duc Dao** – Speaker diving deep into the technical nature of Large Language Models (LLMs)
- **Vy Lam** – Expert speaker on Enterprise AI Systems deployment

### III. Key Highlights from the Sessions

#### 1. Context Is Everything: Making AI Actually Work for You

This session changed the way I view AI, as I realized that a system is only truly useful when it is placed in a clear and complete **context**. The speaker analyzed many real-world situations in which AI produced vague or incorrect responses because users gave overly general prompts and lacked enough input information.

The most striking idea was the **"Second AI Brain"** concept — an intelligent virtual assistant that can remember over time, understand user habits, and accompany them continuously instead of responding only to isolated requests. The methods for optimizing **Context Engineering** and setting up memory were also explained clearly, opening new directions for intelligent AI Agent applications.

#### 2. Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick

I had the chance to explore the **Amazon Quick** toolkit in depth and see the power of AI in extracting and managing enterprise data. Its main features include:

- **Quick Chat Agent**: Interact with and query complex data systems using natural language
- **Quick Flows**: Automate workflows flexibly without requiring extensive programming skills (No-code)
- **Quick Spaces**: A centralized space for securely storing, managing, and sharing internal knowledge
- **Quick Sight**: Transform raw data into visual reports and dashboards with AI support

This topic showed that AI is not only a helper for developers, but also a strategic tool for data analysts and administrators.

#### 3. From Edge To Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation

The role of a Content Delivery Network (CDN) was clarified through **Amazon CloudFront** in improving application performance:

- Minimize response latency and accelerate page loading across the globe
- Reduce bandwidth costs through distributed caching at Edge Locations
- Strengthen security through mandatory HTTPS and AWS WAF integration
- Improve availability and resilience during sudden traffic spikes

This content was very practical and can be directly applied to the **ZeroBug Agent** project I am currently developing.

#### 4. 36 hrs with LotusHacks – Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality

Representatives from **Team VIB** shared their journey of turning an initial idea into a complete product in just 36 hours during the Hackathon. Valuable lessons included:

- Identifying ideas based on real problems and user needs
- Team spirit and collaboration under high time pressure
- Time management, task breakdown, and resource allocation skills

#### 5. Non-Determinism of "Deterministic" LLM Settings

This in-depth session clarified the nature of LLMs when calculating probabilities to choose the next token. The speaker explained the paradox that even with **Temperature = 0**, the system can still return different results across runs. The influencing factors included:

- Objective reasons behind model non-determinism
- The impact of hardware optimization and inference algorithms
- Practical techniques to control and reduce variance during AI product development

#### 6. Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System: The Case of Startup Credit Scoring

The final session introduced a **Multi-Agent** architecture applied to startup credit risk assessment. The **"Virtual Credit Committee"** model impressed me because multiple specialized AI agents discussed and challenged each other before reaching a final decision rather than relying on a single model. The session also offered a practical perspective on data security in the finance and banking sector, the deployment of Multi-Agent systems in production, and operational cost optimization.
