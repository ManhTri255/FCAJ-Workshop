Throughout the 12-week **First Cloud AI Journey** program, I had the opportunity to learn and practice AWS services, progressing from fundamental concepts to real-world infrastructure deployment. At the same time, I participated in building the **ZeroBug Agent** workshop in the role of **TV1 — IAM & VPC Foundation**.

My responsibilities in the workshop included creating **IAM Users** and **IAM Roles** for team members and AWS services, deploying **Amazon VPC**, creating **Public Subnets** and **Private Subnets**, and configuring **Internet Gateway**, **NAT Gateway**, **Route Tables**, and **Security Groups** to establish the networking and security foundation for the entire system. After completing the work, I handed over infrastructure details such as **VPC ID, Subnet ID, Security Group, and IAM Role ARN** to the next team members so they could continue deploying **Amazon S3, Amazon RDS, Amazon EC2, AWS Lambda, Step Functions, API Gateway, CloudFront, and AWS WAF**. Through this process, I strengthened my skills in AWS network architecture design, access control management with IAM, and implementing infrastructure following a Public/Private Subnet model.

In terms of work ethic, I always tried to complete my tasks according to schedule, actively studied the official AWS documentation and workshop materials whenever I encountered issues related to IAM, VPC, NAT Gateway, Route Tables, and Security Groups, and ensured that other team members could deploy their work smoothly on the same infrastructure.
